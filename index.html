What is Web Accessibility ??

In plain english ...

Let's first explain the basics of what it is and why it keeps being discussed. Web Accessibility increases everyone's ability to have access to important information and critical data needed to manage their day to day activities.

The plain and simple answer is that all internet content should be displayed in a way that allows everybody to use it. A huge amount of the content on the web is critical and vital to people's everyday lives! In today's world, the internet is a fountain of vital and life-sustaining information that everybody drinks from.

Why is Web Accessibility Important ??

In plain english ...

Unfortunately, many people are not able to physically search and retrieve content because of the way the software programs have been coded and built.

Today, we are in new waters with aids like screen readers and other assistive technologies.

Who is effected by Web Accessibility ??

In plain english ...

Some of the physical and mental disabilities that people live with are blindness/braille, sign language, deaf/hearing impaired, mental cognitive disabilities, and media that is difficult to access found in the form of print, visual, and audio .

Most of us socialize around people everyday who live with handicaps or disabilities. However, many people who do not fit the common stereotypes of "handicapped or disabiled" still suffer internet access difficuties. Those people are handicapped because of physical injuries, mental injuries (concussions, etc), illnesses, car accidents, strokes, heart attacks, diseases, arthritis, wrist/hand/arm impairments, visual or audio injuries, etc.

Its a Brave New World out here folks! In a split second, anything can happen to any of you, at any time to disable or handicap your ability to access vital and critical web content. As you may be beginning to see ... we are all in this "internet thingy" together. What effects you effects me!

The Pros and Cons of Web Accessibility

1) Over 1 billion people are handicapped and disabled, worldwide

In plain english ...

Believe it or not, between the World Bank.org , the W3.org , and, the World Health Organization.org , it is estimated that 15% to 20% of this planet, or, over 1 billion people, have handicaps or disabilities that significantly limit their access to critical information and data from the internet which is needed to guide and operate their daily lives.

2) ADA Web Accessibility Lawsuits against businessesrs

In plain english ...

Lawsuit filings are rapidly increasing because much information and data on the web is actually life-sustaining and vital to the daily maintence of people's lives. Beyonce was recently sued over her website because it alledgedly violated ADA regulations. It seems nowadays that information and data is so important, by so many people, that accessibility lawsuits are becoming more commonplace in the corporate world. In 2018, there were over 1000 lawsuits filed related to web accessibility, according to the BOIA.org.

Some of the industries being sued include clothing and apparel, telecommunications, restaurants, consumer goods, and e-commerce. Some blame the US Department of Justice for lack of clear guidelines on web access compliance. The US DOJ is responsible for compliance. The DOJ is responsible for compliance of the Americans with Disabilities Act signed into law on July 26, 1990, by then President George HW Bush.

3) A Profitable Business Case for Web Accessibility

In plain english ...

A mind-boggling paradox for this entire issue of lack of sufficient web accessibility is the business case some are making that supports improving web accessibility. The narrative for that business case says it is estimated that 1 billion people with various disabilities and handicaps ironically represent over 6 trillion dollars in spending power. That narrative, if true, is a huge deal-breaker for any company, large or small, who is ignoring or sitting on the sidelines of this problem.

This is a window of opportunity for some companies, corporations, government agencies, and global ngo's to eliminate this "lack of web access problem". In turn, the winners and problem solvers will acquire many life-long loyal customers who appreciate that you solved a "very good problem" by enabling them to release $6 trillion dollars of spending power.

Web Access Problems awaiting Problem-Solvers

In plain english ...

WebAIM did a survey of the homepage of the top 1 million websites, worldwide. It is discussed in the WebAIM Million report. 97.8% of the home pages had detectable WCAG-2 failures. 5 of 6 pages were due to improper HTML code for Accessibility. By the way, the automated tool used is only capable detecting a portion of a actual accessibility issues and errors.

Web developers who are serious about web accessibility can be members of "Teach Accessibility" and learn more about Web Accessibility from them with their Web Accessibility Tutorial. It is compiled by a group of tech companies. Some of the companies are Apple, Google, LinkedIn, Adobe, and Microsoft.

The Office of Disability Employment Policy (US Department of Labor) has a partnership with "Teach" and also funds a program called PEAT (Partnership on Employment and Accessible Technology).
